## Description

This Study is directed to previously unidentified prior art references,
including patent and non-patent literature.

Looking for a device with blades used for blending, mixing, and/or chopping
the contents of a container, preferably for blending foodstuffs. Interested
in rechargeable devices for the home (blenders, food processors) as well as
industrial devices. Main points that must be shown:

- a set of blades or a rotor;
- a container;
- the blades rotate around a rotational axis and blend the contents of the
  container;
- the rotational axis of the blade is offset from the **longitudinal axis of
  the container** (not the longitudinal axis of the device base);
- the offset is between **5% and 15%** of the diameter of the blades.

This provides a "tornado effect" for better blending (see study patent Fig. 9).

## Research Requirements

**RR1** — To satisfy, complete all 3 sub-requirements (priority requirement).
A blender configured to blend foodstuffs, comprising a base assembly, a
container assembly, and control circuitry, further including:

- **RR1.1** — a set of blades configured to rotate around a rotational axis
  and blend foodstuffs, wherein the set of blades has a blade diameter during
  blending by the blender;
- **RR1.2** — the rotational axis of the blade is offset from the
  longitudinal axis of the container body;
- **RR1.3** — the offset is between 5% and 15% of the diameter of the blade.

## Hard filters

- Single reference meeting RR1.1 + RR1.2 + RR1.3 together is strongly
  preferred over piecemeal matches.
- Offset must be measured against the **container's** longitudinal axis, not
  the base's — do not accept references that only show base-offset designs.
- Numeric offset (5%-15% of blade diameter) must be verifiable or reasonably
  inferable from drawings/dimensions — do not guess.

## Known reference points

- Study patent figures show the offset producing a tornado/vortex effect
  during blending (Fig. 9).
