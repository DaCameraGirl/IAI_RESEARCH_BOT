 Description
This Study is directed to previously unidentified prior art references, including patent and non-patent literature.

The objective of this Study is to find a disclosure of the following molecule:

       Screenshot_2026-06-16_at_2.01.30_PM

Common Name: Oximidol 

INCI Name: METHYLOXETANECARBAMIDO THIAZOLYL RESORCINOL 

CAS No.: 3081328-14-2 

Oximidol is a tyrosinase inhibitor compound, which may also have been known as KT-939, KWhite 939, or NexaWhite 939 and is used in skin care compositions for brightening and spot-correcting.  The compound reduces melanin production by inhibiting tyrosinase, and can be used to reduce dark spots or uneven skin tones . This can be used as a cosmetic replacement for older brightening agents such as arbutin, kojic acid, niacinamide, hydroquinone, and thiamidol.  It may also be used for antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

It may be present in anti-freckle creams; hair care preparations; skin conditioning creams; sunscreen cream; body lotion; skin whitening creams; teeth whitening preparations; age spot reducing creams; and the like.

We are also interested in seeing an association of the molecule Oximidol with Isopropyl Lauroyl Sarcosinate (solvent to solubilize the molecule) with a priority date before August 18 2023?

Please see Study Patent for additional details.
