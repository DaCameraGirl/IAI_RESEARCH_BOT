## Description

This Study is directed to previously unidentified prior art references,
including patent and non-patent literature, disclosing the molecule:

- **Common name:** Oximidol
- **INCI name:** Methyloxetanecarbamido Thiazolyl Resorcinol
- **CAS No.:** 3081328-14-2
- **Also known as:** KT-939, KWhite 939, NexaWhite 939

Oximidol is a tyrosinase inhibitor used in skin-care compositions for
brightening/spot-correcting. It reduces melanin production by inhibiting
tyrosinase and is positioned as a cosmetic replacement for arbutin, kojic
acid, niacinamide, hydroquinone, and thiamidol. Also of interest: antioxidant
and anti-inflammatory effects. Possible product categories: anti-freckle
creams, hair care, skin conditioning creams, sunscreen, body lotion, skin
whitening creams, teeth whitening preparations, age-spot reducing creams.

Also interested in an association of Oximidol with **Isopropyl Lauroyl
Sarcosinate** (solvent to solubilize the molecule) with a priority date
before **2023-08-18**.

## Research Requirements

- **RR1** — Oximidol (exact structure as shown in study patent).
